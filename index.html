ABOUT

Takafumi Tsukui

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow at Research School of Astronomy and Astrophysics (RSAA) in the Australian National University. Read more on the RSAA newsletter.

I did my PhD at Graduate University for Advanced Studies, SOKENDAI and the National Astronomical Observatory of Japan under the supervision of Prof. Satoru Iguchi. You can find my PhD thesis here titled "Formation and Evolution of Galactic Structures Using Gas and Stellar Kinematics".

I obtained my Bachelor in Physics at Tohoku University (Japan).

Research

Galaxy formation and evolution

I am interested in how galaxies have formed and evolved across cosmic time. The motion of star and gas within galaxies give us clues about how galaxies have evolved, how massive they are, and how galaxies will evolve.

My research focus is to elucidate the structure of galaxies from the early Universe to the current Universe, analysing gas and stellar kinematics measured by state-of-the-art telescopes such as the Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array (ALMA) and Very Large Telescope (VLT).
